Market Narratives
New York City / NYC-Newark CBSA
Supply Squeeze: Starts at 14-Year Low
NYC ranked #3 nationally but the pipeline is broken at the starts level. 485x (Apr 2024) imposes union wage floors on 100+ unit projects — zero registered as of late 2024. Multifamily starts hit a 14-year low in Q3 2025 (894 units) even as permit filings surged to 11,746 units that quarter on 485x deadline pressure.
TRD Nov 2025485x Zero Projects
Boston / Boston-Cambridge-Newton CBSA
Decade Low Despite Policy Push
Peaked at 15,019 units (2021), now ~9,000 (2024) — lowest in a decade. Affordable Homes Act (Aug 2024) targets 222,000 units statewide by 2035. High financing costs and MBTA Communities zoning compliance are the headwinds.
Boston Foundation Nov 2025
Miami / Miami–Fort Lauderdale–West Palm Beach CBSA
Post-Boom Normalization
Fell from #13 to #16 nationally. Florida multifamily permits dropped 26.5% statewide. The pandemic construction surge is unwinding; elevated rental vacancy (~8.3%) signals near-term oversupply risk even as population inflows continue.
Census BPS Annual
Chicago / Chicago-Naperville-Elgin CBSA
Top National Performer in 2024
Rose from #22 to #14 nationally (+20.2% YoY). Illinois led all states by % gain (+20.5%). Chicago city added 4,371 units. Public-private mega-projects (The 78, Quantum Shore) driving pipeline that smaller markets can't replicate.

2025 Capital Markets Context
Volume Recovery
Bisnow/CoStar found investors deployed $255B+ across core sectors in 2025, with each quarter exceeding the last. Miami commercial RE sales up 35% YoY. Trophy office CMBS surged as best-in-class towers with creditworthy tenants cleared the market.
Office: Trophy vs. Commodity Divide
Deutsche Bank Center's $1.1B refi and 3 Bryant Park's billion-dollar CMBS deal showed lender appetite for fully-leased Class A. Office CMBS delinquency simultaneously hit 12.34% all-time high. The bifurcation between the top 5% and everything else has never been wider.
Miami: Institutional Conviction Holds
Despite rising vacancy and permit collapse, Simon's $512M Brickell buy and OKO/Oak Row's $520M land record show institutional capital still betting on South Florida urban core at scale. David Simon projected Brickell City Centre could reach $3–4B in 10 years.
What Stays Paywalled
Individual private deal cap rates, sub-$50M transactions, and submarket absorption remain behind CoStar/MSCI/RCA paywalls. TRD, Bisnow, and CO surface the institutional tier where press releases and CMBS rating agency reports (Fitch, KBRA) make data accessible.
